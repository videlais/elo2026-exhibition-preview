# description

*Portraits and Other Riffs* investigates regeneration and remix through hybrid human-algorithmic composition. The project brings the performative versioning of jazz improvisation—attunement to audience, openness to accident—into dialogue with digital environments that invite recalibration and transformation. Each piece begins with a source text that is reworked through layered procedural processes and deliberate hand-editing, producing poems that exist as interface-driven performances. The pieces can be appreciated visually, but they can also be seen as scripts for oral performance.

"Portrait of Charles Bernstein" draws on audio recordings of the poet, first rendered through uncorrected speech-to-text transcription. From this unstable textual field, a poem is carved by hand under a simple acrostic constraint and presented through an animated display the reader can modulate.

"Acrosticizer for Rachel Carson" extends this method but uses the environmentalist's The Sea Around Us as corpus. Lyric variations generated through an interaction with the Collage Poetry Generator are hand-stitched into acrostic sequences that refract Carson's language. Readers choose one to four lyric scripts, which are loaded in the "poetry console," and then make selections to tune how they are revealed on the screen.

"Riffs and Remediations" also begins with a source text; but it reworks original, early internet poems and a critical essay by the author into a three-component animation shaped by algorithmic permutation.

"Collage Poetry Generator" makes the compositional engine visible, inviting viewers to experiment with the generative logic underlying the exhibition by riffing with texts of their own choosing.

The interactive displays (HTML, JavaScript) and Collage Poetry Generator were coded with Claude AI.

# curatorialStatement

Building from algorithmic composition, *Portraits and Other Riffs* gathers an unstable textual field across a series of works — *Portrait of Charles Bernstein*, *Acrosticizer for Rachel Carson*, *Riffs and Remediations*, and *Collage Poetry Generator* — each taking a particular source text and computing a set of possible permutations. Purposely reminiscent of earlier internet poetic forms, the works are presented as "scripts for [an] oral performance," anticipating future, interface-driven performances of the same material.

Invoking the intermediality of their forms, *Portraits and Other Riffs* foregrounds the re-reading and re-writing of existing texts, staging the collage of a processed source for use in the present. In doing so, the work offers a reflection on how digital poetics can continually remix the past, treating existing concepts and phrases as material for new, iterative composition.

# creatorBiography

**Kenneth Sherwood** is a poet with an interest in performance, orality, materiality, remix, and code. He co-edited RIF/T, one of the earliest online journals for e-lit in the 1990s, and did his graduate work in the Poetics Program at SUNY Buffalo. As a critic, he has published on modernist and postmodern poets from Louis Zukofsky and Mina Loy to Cecilia Vicuna and Kamau Brathwaite. His print poems have appeared in several chapbooks, including *Code of Signals* (Moria, 2017). He is a professor in Language, Literature and Writing at Indiana University of Pennsylvania.
