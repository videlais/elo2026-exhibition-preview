# description

*BUGCAT* is a series of smart contracts deployed on the Ethereum blockchain. Each contract embodies a historically significant vulnerability (reentrancy, integer overflow, unprotected initialization, predictable randomness, a misspelled constructor) as executable logic: the flaw is the work itself, live and callable on-chain. Each cat is bound to a real historical contract (The DAO, BEC Token, Parity Wallet, FoMo3D, Rubixi), the ancestor of its wound.

Made as Ethereum reaches its tenth year, the series commemorates the platform through the scars of its past, the catastrophic failures that produced today's security consciousness. Under the theme "(Un)Supervised," BUGCAT sits in the tension between two regimes of control. The Ethereum Virtual Machine executes without supervision, autonomously and indifferently. The same code demands the most rigorous human supervision: the line-by-line scrutiny of the auditor.

The work is grounded in verificational poetics, a practice of reading code as an act of auditing and testing. BUGCAT stages this reading as three audits, offered for each cat.

Reading the source is a structural audit. The reader studies where the logic fails and meets an aesthetic of accurate ambiguity: a line syntactically exact yet functionally unpredictable, because formal logic and the actual behavior of the EVM do not coincide.

remember() recalls the cat's memory. It queries whether the cat's historical ancestor still exists on-chain, a temporal audit that situates the flaw within the blockchain's timeline.

caress() is a functional audit. To read a vulnerability fully is to understand the state change it permits and to enact it. In the exhibition interface this enactment is performed on the reader's behalf, surfacing the Meow event as on-chain proof that the failure was reached. Performing this enactment by one's own hand is the furthest reach of this reading, and it lies on the same continuous path: from here the reader goes to bugcat.org, learns the contract, composes the transaction, signs it, and sends it.

The primary medium is on-chain executable code (Solidity). The browser interface (HTML, CSS, JavaScript) serves as a reading instrument. It renders source, recalls history, and stages each proof. It is the near end of a path completed only by the reader's own execution.

# creatorBiography

**Zeroichi Arakawa** is an artist and doctoral researcher whose practice and research are situated at the intersection of code poetry, media archaeology, and software studies.

Their current work centers on the development of "Verification Poetics," a theoretical and creative framework that reframes the act of reading source code as an act of auditing, testing, and institutional verification. Through the creation of on-chain artworks on the Ethereum blockchain, they explore the concept of "accurate ambiguity"—the inherent tension between the formal precision of code and the unpredictable, physical, and historical realities of its execution.

Arakawa's projects, such as *BUGCAT* and *DeepSea*, serve as critical case studies in investigating the linguistic limits and the materiality of decentralized infrastructures. They are particularly interested in how the "scars" of software history—vulnerabilities and bugs—can be re-read as poetic moments that define our contemporary digital identity.
