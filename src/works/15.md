# description

Set in the year 3099 AD, *Digital USTC* delves into the speculative fusion of artificial intelligence and human legacy. It constructs a narrative framework where digital beings embark on a cross-temporal exploration of the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC) campus, retracing the symbiotic memories and conceptual "genes" shared between humanity and its digital progeny.

The work establishes an ecosystem powered by Al-assisted algorithms, designed to weave together students'scientific imagination with the enduring humanism. Users are invited to generate personalized digital avatars with accompanying Al-composed Chinese/English poetry, then immerse themselves in a first-person, nonlinear exploration of a digitally reconstructed USTC.

The experience unfolds across a collaboratively built, dynamically iterating campus map. The East Campus welcomes users with the base of our electronic literature research group and fully-booked study rooms in the library, while Mr. Guo Moruo, the first President of USTC, serves as the Al poet-librarian and shares histories of the campus from Guo Maruo Square to the Cherry Blossom Avenue, in a poetic and romantic style.

The West Campus interface begins with a transcendent dialogue: a face-to-face conversation with an Al simulation of the revered scientist Qian Xuesen. Guided by this digital entity, users will explore a secret imaginary laboratory beneath Ye Xi Lake. We will find the lab password through the 1958 Cafe, and then start a scientific tour of the Life Sciences Laboratory, the Fire Science Laboratory, and the Synchrotron Radiation Laboratory.

The Middle Campus further adopts a game-like structure, where users must interact with NPCs to gather clues and find hidden exits, playfully blending daily student life with whimsical scientific puzzles. To technically enable this complex narrative, the work proposes an innovative modular architecture: Website-Informed Neural Networks (WINNs). Inspired by Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs), WINNs address "development depth" challenges by structuring the work as a network of interconnected, conditionally-activated thematic websites. User interaction triggers dynamic routing between these sites, mimicking neural network propagation and creating a distributed, navigable narrative. This makes WINNs both a technical framework and a metaphorical model for complex interactive storytelling and distributed cognition.

Through the bilingual design and cross-cultural narratives, the work elevates USTC culture into a global story of youth, knowledge, and continuity. It demonstrates a deep fusion of technological concept, humanistic inquiry, and artistic expression via digital immersion and aims to retrace the unavoidable evolution of hypertext literature— from nonlinear narratives to intelligent interaction— while pioneering new forms of Al-era literary creation in China. It positions digital technology as a bridge connecting past and future, reality and imagination, striving to express both the local culture and humanity's enduring quest for meaning beyond the confines of time and space.

# curatorialStatement

*Digital USTC* sets its story in the distant future of 3099 AD. In a post-apocalyptic setting, the work asks the player to create a robotic self and explore the campus of the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC) and its cast of characters. Through a chatbox interface, the player converses with these characters, posing questions that a language model parses and answers.

Echoing the parser-based works of the past, in which typed commands drive progress, *Digital USTC* connects that lineage to the present by offering open textboxes for longer sentences and free-form questions. In using a game-like structure to route dynamically between sections of a narrative shaped by both player input and model generation, the work sketches one possible route for electronic literature: generated, nondeterministic digital worlds—offering a reflection on how model-driven systems reshape narrative authority.

# creatorBiography

**Dr. Licheng Xie** graduated from the Department of English at Lancaster University, UK, and currently serves as a lecturer at the University of Science and Technology of China. Her main research areas include American literature and digital media arts, with published works such as "The Digital Physics of Reading DeLillo" and "DeLillo's Language as Visual Art in the Digital Age." She also contributed to the digital literary work "Peaceful Dream" by Norwegian artist Ottar Ormstad, which was included in the 4thvolume of Electronic Literature Organization. Her ongoing projects explore the intersection of technology and humanities through digital literary creation and research. She leads an electronic literature student team at USTC, exploring the use of AI-assisted creation of bilingual Chinese-English digital literary works.
