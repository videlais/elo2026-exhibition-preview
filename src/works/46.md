# description

This digital fiction written in Twine utilises multi-media to augment the narrative. The story draws upon literature, science and philosophy to create an imaginative world and characters. Its premise is based around two characters who must face their inner demons and trust each other to achieve a goal. There is an underlying theme of political power and fight for humanity. In the story giant scorpions have evolved to have human thought but lack souls which they are working to take from humans. A reader might interpret theses scorpions as metaphors for AI. I have used Twine as a media that can enhance a reader experience through interaction, visual elements and sound. While there are game-like elements the focus is on the written narrative with a story arc as opposed to the reader choosing their path. However, there is choice in how the story is read. For instance the reader can choose which of the main two characters they wish to follow; but to fully experience the story they must then follow the other. As it is relatively long I opted to break the story into two parts with the second being The Butterfly and the Tree. In this second part the two characters are now together and the reader switches POV between the them. This Twine is thus an experiment using the second person 'you' POV across two characters using background colour themes to differentiate. These colour themes subtly change as the characters develop. Plus there is a background auditor or storyteller who drops in at times. I describe the story as star-like in that the reader can choose to follow links that can give additional elements to the story such as character memories, follow bad choices to consider consequences, as well as offering links to the aforementioned literature, science and philosophy that informed the narrative. It is meant to encourage thinking. I would like to acknowledge my friend Jimi Bellaney for providing the original soundtrack. Otherwise all the work is my own. Thank you, Carolijn.
