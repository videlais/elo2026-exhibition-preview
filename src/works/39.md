# description

*The Grand Hotel Bald Cockatoo* is a work-in-progress, the third work in *Visitations: The Grand Hotels (of Joseph Cornell)*. The series extends the imaginary universe of Joseph Cornell's collage series, *The Grand Hotels*, and the late Robert Coover's book *The Grand Hotels of Joseph Cornell*. Visitations explore different modalities of narrative expression in virtual and augmented environments and are also an exploration of how emerging AI platforms will play a role in the workflow of future immersive reality experiences. The Grand Hotel Bald Cockatoo also builds upon the authors' prior work in combinatory cinema, virtual, and augmented reality.

Where the first two works in *Visitations* are situated in AR and VR environments, *The Grand Hotel Bald Cockatoo* will be an immersive embodied experience. Based on perhaps the most absurd of Robert Coover's Grand Hotel sketches, this hotel is a hotel of dreams, in which the hotel manager is a fortunetelling cockatoo. The audience will enter a large installation in the form of a bird's nest. Embedded in the walls of the nest will be four large screens, in which fragments of dream narratives recombine. These dream narratives were written by the authors in an adapted exquisite corpse fashion inspired by details of Coover's original text and prompted to AI text-to-video generation engines. Voice-over narration of selected narratives, tracked separately from the videos, will fill the room. At the back of the nest, one will find a curtained booth, which houses both a video of the fortune-teller and an interactive fortune-telling device. The cockatoo addresses the audience member sitting in the booth while spouting off randomly generated fortunes. The user can press a button to generate a fortune of their own, which will be printed for them to take home.

This online exhibition for the ELO 2026 (Un)Supervised Conference in July 2026 is a kind of *wunderkammer*, a cabinet of curiosities, a toolset of as-yet-unassembled parts for an immersive work in the process of becoming. Because ELO 2026 is an online conference, the installation will be represented through a portfolio of samples of its components, including concept sketches, video tracks, audio tracks, and an online representation of the fortune-telling machine.

# curatorialStatement

*The Grand Hotel Bald Cockatoo* offers a rare opportunity to encounter an immersive narrative in the midst of its formation. Rather than presenting a finished installation, the work foregrounds process, inviting audiences to engage with the conceptual, aesthetic, and technical components through which a complex mixed-reality experience is assembled. Sketches, media fragments, textual materials, and interactive elements collectively reveal the generative ecology of contemporary electronic literature.

Building upon longstanding conversations between literary experimentation, visual art, and digital narrative, the project extends a lineage of combinatory storytelling into emerging computational media. Its playful juxtaposition of dreams, chance operations, and algorithmic generation reflects on authorship as an increasingly distributed practice, where creative agency is negotiated among collaborators, computational systems, and audiences.

The exhibition format itself becomes significant. By exposing the work's constituent parts rather than concealing them within a seamless immersive experience, *The Grand Hotel Bald Cockatoo* encourages reflection on prototyping as a critical and creative methodology. It reminds us that electronic literature is as much about experimentation and iteration as completed artefacts, demonstrating how works in progress can illuminate the evolving relationships between narrative, technology, and artistic practice.

# creatorBiography

**Roderick Coover** is a filmmaker, media artist, and scholar working in interactive documentary and immersive narrative. He is Director of the MFA Program in Film and Media Arts at Temple University. His projects span films, museum installations, print publications, and multimedia collaborations combining fictional and nonfiction storytelling. Coover's works include *Unknown Territories*, *The Theory of Time Here*, and *Cultures in Webs*, and he is the co-editor of *Switching Codes: Thinking Through Digital Technologies in the Humanities and Arts*. His research explores relationships between digital technologies, social sciences, and the humanities, with particular attention to visual geography, word-image relations, and interactive narrative forms.

**Scott Rettberg** is Professor of Digital Culture at the University of Bergen and Director of the Center for Digital Narrative, a Norwegian Centre of Research Excellence. He is a co-founder and former executive director of the Electronic Literature Organization and the author or co-author of many electronic literary works and films such as *The Unknown*, *Kind of Blue*, *Implementation,* and *Riverrun (fascistchicfascistquick).* His creative and scholarly work has been exhibited internationally, including at major art venues and online exhibitions. Rettberg's research concerns electronic literature, digital culture, and computational narrative, and he has led major research initiatives such as the ELMCIP project and knowledge base.

**Caitlin Fisher** is a Canadian media artist, writer, and Professor of Cinema and Media Arts at York University, where she directs the Immersive Storytelling Lab and the Augmented Reality Lab. Her research and creative practice focus on immersive storytelling, electronic literature, augmented and virtual reality, and experimental narrative media. Fisher is internationally known for pioneering work in digital literature and augmented-reality narrative and for creating one of the earliest AR poems. She has served as President of the Electronic Literature Organization and previously held a Canada Research Chair in Digital Culture and a Fulbright Research Chair.
