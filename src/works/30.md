# description

*The Red Manifest* is an interactive digital storytelling experience that invites audiences into a mysterious journey through the atmospheric landscapes of Transylvania. Developed using the ink narrative scripting language, the project combines literary writing, player agency, and immersive sound design to create a story that is not simply read, but actively experienced.

Inspired by the rich folklore, myths, and cultural history of Transylvania, Red Manifest reimagines storytelling as an exploratory process. Rather than following a fixed, linear path, players navigate the narrative through choices and discoveries that shape the rhythm of the experience. Fragments of text, environmental details, and branching dialogues gradually reveal a larger mystery, encouraging curiosity and rewarding attentive exploration.

The use of the ink JavaScript engine allows the story to unfold dynamically. Players influence the progression of events through their decisions, creating a personalized narrative journey that reflects their own interpretations and priorities. This branching structure transforms readers into participants, giving them an active role in constructing meaning and engaging with the world around them.

Sound design plays an equally important role in the experience. Ambient audio, environmental effects, and carefully crafted musical elements work alongside the written narrative to deepen immersion and emotional impact. The distant sounds of forests, echoes within abandoned spaces, and subtle shifts in atmosphere guide the player's attention and reinforce the tone of each moment. Audio becomes more than accompaniment. It functions as an essential storytelling tool that shapes tension, anticipation, and emotional connection.

*Red Manifest* explores the unique possibilities of digital media by blending elements of literature, interactive fiction, and game design. It challenges traditional distinctions between books and games, demonstrating how stories can emerge through participation rather than passive consumption. The project invites audiences to slow down, pay attention to sensory details, and uncover meaning through interaction.

At its core, *Red Manifest* is an experiment in narrative design and a reflection on how technology can expand the ways we tell stories. It highlights the potential of interactive fiction to foster empathy, curiosity, and wonder through active engagement.

The *Red Manifest* is a literary experience shaped by choice, atmosphere, and sound. It invites players to wander through the shadows of Transylvania and discover that some of the most powerful stories are those we help bring to life ourselves.

# curatorialStatement

*The Red Manifest* conjures the folklore of *Dracula* (1897) as a hypertextual adventure built using the narrative scripting language ink, placing the player in the first-person position of the Count as Jonathan Harker, Abraham Van Helsing, and Renfield arrive, in turn, at the door of his home. Following each character through a set of branching choices, the work retells the familiar tale from the predator's vantage rather than the victims'.

In foregrounding the complicity of limited choice, *The Red Manifest* turns the tyranny of the interface into its subject. The player is obliged to take positions they may find distasteful such as acting as the Count against the very protagonists they know from the source as the "I" driving the story implicates the reader in its menace. The work offers a reflection on how branching structures bind agency to complicity, making the player answerable for choices the interface all but compels.

# creatorBiography

**Joao de Mendonca Salim** is a current GTA at the UCF's Digital Media M.A. program, he is a digital media developer and interactive artist based in Orlando, Florida. Joao's work frequently explores the intersection of programming, interactive design, and unconventional narrative structures.
