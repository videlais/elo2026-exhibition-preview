# description

*Dreamscapes* is a web-based narrative reflecting on relationships between experience and memory by referencing liminal spaces between dreaming and waking.

The work begins when the title word is clicked. As new lines of the narrative play out, subsequent lines dissolve, similar to the nature of recalling elements of a dream upon waking and having those same, very real and perhaps felt elements, disappear as consciousness blooms in the waking mind. The narrative and imagery move between waking and dreaming inviting the reader to ponder the tangibility of thoughts, the magic of mind, and the fractures between parsing and knowing.

# curatorialStatement

*Dreamscapes* explores the affordances of digital poetry through a minimalist interface that foregrounds emergence, transformation, and ephemerality. Activated through a simple interaction, the work generates a sequence of dreamlike textual fragments that appear, fade, repeat, and overwrite one another. The work draws upon qualities often associated with dreaming itself: discontinuity, associative logic, repetition, and the fluid movement between imagery, emotions, and states of being. The digital medium is not merely a delivery mechanism for poetic text but an active participant in its construction. The appearance, disappearance, and transformation of language use temporality and motion as compositional elements. *Dreamscapes* offers a meditation on memory, imagination, and the instability of language itself.

# creatorBiography

**Nanette Wylde** is an interdisciplinary artist, writer and cultural worker making environmental, socially reflective, and language-based works using a variety of hybrid media. Her artist books, prints, and electronic works are included in significant international collections. Wylde's interests include: language, personality, difference, beliefs, systems, ideas, movement, reflection, identity, perceptions, structures, stories, socialization, definitions, context, memory, experience, the natural world, change, and residue. She has a BA in Behavioral Science from San José State University. Her MFA is in Interactive Multimedia and Printmaking from Ohio State University. preneo.org/nwylde
