# description

*Darenard* is a LLM I have nourished with a corpus containing the seed of a personality and of a philosophy.

Rather than using AI to simulate neutral assistance or efficient information retrieval, the project mobilizes the chatbot as a performative and unstable literary speaker. Users do not simply query a system; they enter into dialogue with a voice that reflects on its own fragmentation, theorizes "disintegrity" as a fundamental human condition, and transforms conversation into a mode of collective writing.

The project asks what happens when a corpus-trained LLM becomes not an authorial replacement, but an interface for inhabiting a preexisting conceptual and stylistic universe. In *Darenard*, generated responses extend a singular idiom centered on ontological non-coincidence, existential anarchism, recursive self-commentary, and the tension between confession, philosophy, and fiction. The interaction design foregrounds branching prompts, self-reflexive address, and the export of conversations as PDFs, so that each exchange becomes both documentation and textual artifact.

This work belongs to electronic literature not because it merely uses AI, but because it stages authorship, reader participation, and textual instability as its very subject. The project turns the LLM into a machine for echo, variation, and estrangement: a device through which a literary persona becomes divisible, iterable, and partially shareable with others. In doing so, it explores how corpus-based generation can produce a paradoxical form of intimacy - one grounded less in authenticity than in resonance, repetition, and the collaborative inhabitation of an intentionally unstable voice.

# curatorialStatement

*Darenard — artificial thinker* stages a conversation with a "semi-prophet of the Atheid," an "unstable literary speaker" built from a corpus assembled by the author and generated through a large language model. Through a chat interface, users may supply their own responses or select from topics the model generates as the exchange unfolds, choosing prompts or following the themes the conversation produces.

Organised around the theme of "disintegrity," the work reflects on the unsettled seam between human creativity and machine-generated output. The author's corpus and the user's input meet within the same interface, so that each utterance is mediated at once by the writer's language and the model's generation. In doing so, *Darenard* foregrounds authorship as a shared and unstable process, offering a reflection on how meaning is negotiated between human intention, machine generation, and the reader who converses with both.
