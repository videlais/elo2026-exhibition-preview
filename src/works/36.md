# description

<em>Snow Angel's Wheel of Fortune</em> is a playful, chance-based digital interface for when you've got a nasty case of Chicken Little Syndrome—the doom-scrolling, catastrophe-curious, always-bracing-for-impact version of ourselves that can't stop anticipating collapse. Designed as a web-native work of electronic literature, Snow Angel uses aromachology (the art and science of scent and mood), tarot-inflected cosmology, and hypertext ritual to interrupt that spiral and invite (un)supervised wandering, spiritual inquiry, and creative experimentation within a carefully curated digital ecosystem.</p><p>At its core, Snow Angel's Wheel of Fortune is a digital remix engine and ritual instrument. The interface centers a spinning wheel divided into four quadrants—Frankincense, Orange, Peppermint, Almond—each aligned with a cardinal direction, an element, and an archetypal symbol (doorway, flaming heart, crossroads, threshold). Each spin "deals" a fortune that opens into a miniature archive of rituals, symbols, recipes, and essay-ettes informed by artists and thinkers whose work has accompanied me through seasons of personal, professional and creative pivoting. These voices—Nina Simone, Solmaz Sharif, Debbie Millman, Ross Gay, Flannery O'Connor, Vincent Van Gogh, Nicole Krauss, Bianca Giaever, and more—are not arranged as a grand theoretical argument, but as a cross-genre, cross-temporal, cross-cultural, cross-pollinated conversation across sensibilities, histories, and aesthetics.</p><p>The work alchemizes traditional ritual technologies (Tarot, astrology) with contemporary web practices (hyperlinking, randomization, archival curation) to create an accessible digital ecology of care. This structure directly engages the conference theme (Un)Supervised by asking what kinds of supervision we actually need in the age of AI companions, content moderation, and platform-driven attention—offering, instead of automated comfort, a set of symbolic actions and imaginative prompts that return users to their own bodies and senses.</p>

# creatorBiography

Emily Carr writes and teaches in Gulf Coast Florida. She founded the Low-Residency MFA at Oregon State University - Cascades and the BA in Creative Writing at the New College of Florida. Emily is the author of four books of poetry and the creator of <em>Dr. Carr's Word Forage: a poetry boardgame</em> and <em>Carrie Do Wrong's Wheel of Fortune: the World's Best Advice</em>. Her McSweeney's collection, <em>whosoever has let a minotaur enter them, or a sonnet— </em>inspired a beer of the same name, sporadically available at the Ale Apothecary in Bend, Oregon.
