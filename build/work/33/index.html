Alphavision

Description

Alphavision is a narrative game about the impact of “alpha male” and red-pill culture on people of all genders, particularly in the context of dating. The work places the player in the position most aggressively targeted by manosphere influencers: an insecure young man desperate for approval, particularly from women. The protagonist, Claude, activates a pair of “Alphavision” smart glasses in hopes of impressing his date, Mary. Once activated, the glasses introduce the Alpha Coach, whose goal is to steer Claude away from sincerity and toward the tactics of pickup artists and red-pill influencers, including negging, frame control, and competitive posturing. Through satire, the project examines how algorithmically amplified gender ideologies corrupt genuine connection and reinforce arbitrary power structures. Alphavision translates these ideas into interactive systems. Within the game, Alphavision appears as a persistent overlay, an intrusive layer of instructional text and scoring feedback that competes with Claude's ongoing conversation. The Alpha Coach assigns a running “Date Score,” turning each exchange into a metric that can be optimized. Inspired by dating simulation mechanics in which correct dialogue choices accumulate affection points, Alphavision inverts the formula. The higher the Date Score, earned by following the Coach's advice, the more likely it is that Mary will end the date.

The project incorporates a multimedia component through red decoder glasses that the game was designed to be played with. When worn, the glasses tint the player's view in red, partially obscuring detail while revealing information embedded in the overlay's text. This effect reinforces the narrative metaphor that immersion in manosphere logic narrows perception. The player may choose whether to activate the in-game Alphavision glasses and whether to wear the physical decoder glasses, but each choice alters the experience. Supervision in Alphavision is both imposed by the Alpha Coach's constant feedback and accepted by Claude in his search for approval.

Additional interface elements, including app icons for Instagram, Snapchat, and X, along with a visible “Beta-Male ID,” situate the interaction within a broader ecology of platform culture. Rather than measuring specific traits, the system collapses a complex social exchange into a single fluctuating score, echoing how digital platforms quantify attention and self-worth without fully revealing their criteria.

For online exhibition, Alphavision is deployed to Itch.io and designed to function within standard browser environments. The Alpha Coach remains a persistent overlay within the interface, maintaining the sense that another system is always present, watching and scoring. While the original gallery installation incorporated physical red decoder glasses, the web version includes a “decoder mode” setting that allows players to toggle the obscured text effect, preserving the conceptual logic of the physical installation while adapting to web constraints. The project was developed in Unity, with digital art assets created in Procreate. No generative or agentic AI systems are used in the dialogue. The branching narrative is fully authored.

Through narrative design, interface layering, and mixed media elements, Alphavision examines how digital systems shape identity formation. By turning ideology into an intrusive mechanic, the work highlights how easily connections can be distorted when filtered through metrics and hierarchy, and asks what might change if the glasses are removed.

Biography

Michael Cabrera is a graduate student and TA in Game Development at the University of Texas at Dallas. He works as a narrative designer with a focus on interactive storytelling that examines masculinity, power structures, and the cultural impact of digital platforms. His projects explore how games narratives can shape identity and perception through critical design. Portfolio: https://thefrug.github.io/
