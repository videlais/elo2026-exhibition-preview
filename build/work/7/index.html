"A Last Goodbye"

Description

"A Last Goodbye" contextualizes the traditional elements of storytelling within a new, and dynamic, approach to reader engagement. A massive revision of an older story (originally written in 2024-2025), "A Last Goodbye" has a different title and more fully developed action, conflict, and characters. The interactivity has been improved with a wider and more engaging variety of user interactions, all of which "pull" against, or with, the story events. The visual reinforcement of plot and theme has been enhanced with not only new images and animations, but "broken" and interrupted textual spaces that reflect the distant isolation of the narrator, and the reader's often frustrated attempts to learn his story. This work is a good example of how a narrative can be realized through online multimedia, and how traditional story elements, recontextualized within the affordances of new technologies, still persist in electronic literature. This work is made with HTML5, JavaScript & CSS and is playable on desktop, laptop, and portable devices, including phones.
