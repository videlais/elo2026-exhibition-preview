The Two Deaths of Translucent Tim

Description

Once upon a layer of time, late in the 21st century, and in the last years of a failing World Wide Web, there was an aging techno-visionary, or philosopher, or engineer. He was a genius. Or a hack. Some believed him to be AI. Most just never heard of him. Call him Tim. He has spent over five reclusive decades developing a system that not only links together the totality of human textual production, but also, by leveraging breakthroughs in the hard problem of consciousness, can record and store human intentionality within it. But it remains flawed and incomplete. Tim now faces his final days – yet his determination appears infinite. To ensure that his project would continue after he was gone (assuming he was actually ever there), the engineer leaves behind an ailing prototype and a memoir in progress, though it's not immediately clear which is which. And he grants access to one person and one person alone – someone he does not know well but trusts to the core: you. And you would be a fool not to follow this strange intertwingled pathway to the end. But it will be a challenge. You too have some issues.

***

The Two Deaths of Translucent Tim is an ode to a past generation of technologists who dreamed up “hypertext” and its universalizing ethos of deep interconnection. At the same time, it serves as a meditation on the mechanisms that perpetuate division and disillusionment in the present day. It is a work of fiction. Its characters are composites of those who have loved and inspired us.

Themes include: two-deaths philosophy; cognitive entropy; magical spaces; techno-cults; and truthiness.

The interactive digital narrative adopts translucency as a structural and narrative motivation. The Two Deaths of Translucent Tim takes the form of a translucent reading interface in which readers navigate through several different stacks of layers.

Inspired by retro translucent technological artifacts, the HTML5 interface uses React and TypeScript to engineer the experience of moving through text in an alternative and unconventional yet physically intuitive way.

The Two Deaths of Translucent Tim, more specifically, explores how translucency manifests materially, ideologically, and ontologically – and asks what it means to be or become translucent to each other.

Biography

David Ciccoricco is Professor of English at the University of Otago in Dunedin, New Zealand. His research draws on cognitive science and philosophy of mind to establish how fictional minds help explain actual ones. David is the author of Reading Network Fiction (2007), a book on pre-Web and Web-based digital fiction, and Refiguring Minds in Narrative Media (2015), which explores cognitive approaches to narratives in print novels, digital fictions, and story-driven video games. In collaboration with Mez Breeze and Marina Cone, he is the creator of BabyHex, a digital narrative masquerading as a language-learning simulator embodied in a virtual baby. Leslie Xin is an artist and software engineer based in New York City, originally from Toronto, Canada. Her online work centers on web experiments that explore themes of digital connection and intimacy. Offline, she uses oil painting to examine modern relationships with technology and nostalgia. Her work has been recognized by outlets including the Fast Company, The Pudding, and CBC. She also co-leads Unraveling the Algorithm in collaboration with Lulu Liu, a workshop series examining the relationship between textile making and programming.
