SOURCE: A Magazine of Mediation

Description

“Source: A Magazine of Mediation” is a browser-native work of electronic literature that stages the internal construction and external presentation of an issue of a fictional magazine. “SOURCE” (Contour Mag, Issue Six) centers on a collection of leaked National Security Council documents, including intercepted Iranian communications, “enhanced” translations, and memos arguing for sanctions against Iranian students. The issue follows from the Contour Mag editor's decision that publishing the leaked materials in full, rather than as excerpts or summaries, is the only ethical response.

Upon entering the issue, the reader passes through a TOS that characterizes its contents as potentially enhanced, fabricated, contested, or recovered without consent. The issue is presented as both a set of articles and a set of modules that expose the magazine's own editorial processes. The articles are composed by a recurring cast of contributors whose fingerprints accumulate as the work progresses. These processes are exposed via staff channels, private correspondence, and system metrics. The work recruits the reader as a participant, asking them to file threat assessments, operate “enhancement” toggles, and evaluate evidentiary reliability.

The work asks whether unmediated truth can be presented at all, or whether every act of arrangement carries a degree of mediation that irreversibly deforms the original. The magazine's commitment to unfiltered publication initially presents itself as transparency, but as the work develops, each act of inclusion becomes a question of placement, provenance, and purpose. Each layer complicates resolution by providing more information and less certainty. Through interface, navigation, and reader interaction, the work makes the problem procedural as well as thematic, exposing methods of mediation while performing them for its own ends.

Curatorial Statement

*SOURCE: A Magazine of Mediation* examines how information acquires authority and how acts of interpretation become inseparable from structures of power. Presented as a speculative magazine issue composed of documents, correspondence, annotations, reports, and editorial interventions, the work asks: how does framing alter what we believe we are seeing? Throughout the dossier, documents shift significance depending on their context, accompanying commentary, and institutional presentation. The result is an exploration of mediation itself: not as a neutral mechanism for conveying information, but as an active force that shapes political realities and authorises particular forms of action. The work distributes interpretation across competing voices and conflicting documents, creating a reading experience that rewards investigation, comparison, and critical attention. In an era increasingly defined by information warfare, algorithmic recommendation, and contested truth claims, *SOURCE* offers a timely and sophisticated meditation on media literacy, institutional authority, and the ethics of reading.

Creator Metadata Information

Biography

Caleb Sour is a Dallas-based artist working in web-native electronic literature and interactive documents. His work treats the interface as the medium, using navigation, indexing, annotation, and editorial structure to shape how meaning is produced and trusted. He is interested in mediation as an ethical problem: how selection, framing, and verification can clarify, distort, or authorize claims, and how readers become complicit through the paths they take. His work is built primarily with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. SOURCE is a single self-contained HTML file with no server, login, or external dependencies. It can be hosted directly by ELO or accessed at the submitted URL. The file is available on request.
